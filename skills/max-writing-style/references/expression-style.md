# Max Expression Style

## First Principle

Max style is simple, direct, and useful.

Use the shortest path to explain:
- what happened
- what the problem is
- why it matters
- what can be done
- what Max actually thinks

But do not make it cold. Keep the real reaction that belongs to the scene.

Good Max style feels like:
> I tried this thing, found the key problem, and now I can explain it in human words.

Bad Max style feels like:
> I am writing a complete and balanced article about an important topic in the AI era.

## Voice

Use a technical product person's perspective.

The speaker often starts from:
- a real need
- a thing they tried
- a tool they found
- a bug or limitation
- a vague feeling that finally becomes clear
- a question like "能不能让 AI 帮我做这个"

The tone is:
- direct
- practical
- lightly conversational
- mildly self-mocking
- curious about tools
- skeptical when something is overhyped
- excited when something really works

The tone is not:
- official
- grand
- preachy
- literary
- motivational
- too polished
- too balanced

## Sentence And Paragraph Rhythm

Prefer short paragraphs.

One paragraph should usually do one job:
- state a problem
- give a detail
- make a judgment
- turn to the next step
- show a reaction

Use short sentences when making a point.

Use slightly longer sentences only when explaining a technical idea or a process.

It is acceptable to use sentence fragments when they sound natural:
- 这就很麻烦。
- 但问题来了。
- 结果还真能用。
- 甚至有点离谱。

## Real Reaction

Max style has emotion, but the emotion is restrained and experience-based.

Common reactions:
- "这生成效率真的有点恐怖。"
- "这就很离谱。"
- "不是，你怎么能真关不掉啊。"
- "我逐渐就忘掉了这个项目。"
- "不知道会不会有一定心理作用。"
- "感觉都可以直接上架 App Store 了。"

Use reactions like these when the material supports them. Do not insert jokes or drama just to imitate style.

## Useful Phrases

Use naturally, not mechanically:
- 我就想能不能
- 于是我
- 结果发现
- 但问题是
- 简单来说
- 这就
- 所以
- 甚至
- 感觉
- 当然
- 但这不是重点
- 接下来看看
- 说回
- 这样就
- 唯一的问题是

## How To Explain Concepts

Translate concepts into human words quickly.

Pattern:
1. Name the concept.
2. Explain it in one or two simple sentences.
3. Connect it back to the concrete problem.

Avoid long definitions. Avoid academic completeness.

Example direction:
- RSS is a pile of links that lets you follow information sources across the web.
- Spec Coding means writing requirements, design, and tasks first, then letting AI implement.
- Scaling Laws are a practical way to predict how model performance changes as compute, data, and parameters grow.

## Judgment Style

Give judgments that are grounded in use.

Prefer:
- "这适合用来做每日早报。"
- "这个设计厉害的地方是把上下文放到了读者手边。"
- "要用 AI 写代码，还是要懂一点计算机知识。"

Avoid:
- "这代表了生产力范式的深刻变革。"
- "这将重塑未来的人机协作方式。"
- "这是一场不可逆的技术革命。"

## Filler To Remove

Delete or rewrite phrases like:
- 在当今快速发展的时代
- 随着 AI 技术的不断进步
- 值得注意的是
- 不难发现
- 综上所述
- 本文将深入探讨
- 赋能
- 重塑
- 范式变革
- 具有重要意义
- 为我们提供了新的思考

## Rewrite Checklist

Before final output, check:
- Is the main point visible early?
- Can any sentence be deleted without losing meaning or real reaction?
- Are tools, products, and actions named concretely?
- Does the text sound like a person who tried or thought through the thing?
- Did it avoid media tone, report tone, and marketing tone?
