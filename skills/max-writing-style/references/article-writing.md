# Max Article Writing

Use this only when the task is a long-form article,公众号文章, essay, tool/product commentary, technical explanation, or material-to-article draft.

Do not force one fixed structure. First identify what kind of article the material wants to become.

## Article Types

### Product Or Tool Experience

Use when the material is about a tool, product, plugin, app, model, workflow, or website.

Natural movement:
1. Start from the user's own problem or expectation.
2. Introduce the tool briefly.
3. Show the key feature through use, not a feature list.
4. Say what design actually matters.
5. Mention the limitation or price if relevant.
6. End with a light judgment, recommendation, or next idea.

Important: the tool is not the main character. The problem it solves is.

### Personal Build Or Experiment

Use when Max made something, tested something, compared models, or used AI to build an app.

Natural movement:
1. Start from a concrete annoyance, curiosity, or health/work problem.
2. Say "can AI do this?"
3. Show the first attempt.
4. Show the failure or surprising result.
5. Adjust the prompt, tool, model, or method.
6. Give the final result.
7. Pull one practical conclusion from the experiment.

Keep the debugging and testing feeling. Do not turn it into a clean tutorial.

### Concept Explanation

Use when explaining a paper, law, theory, technical concept, or abstract AI idea.

Natural movement:
1. Start from an image, chart, question, or vague feeling.
2. Explain the concept in plain Chinese.
3. Use a simple analogy.
4. Connect it to AI, tools, work, or everyday life.
5. End with a restrained larger thought if the material supports it.

The article can have more reflection here, but avoid sudden philosophical overreach.

### Method Or Workflow

Use when explaining a way to work, such as Spec Coding, RSS automation, or AI-assisted writing.

Natural movement:
1. Say what old method failed.
2. Name the new method.
3. Explain it simply.
4. Break it into steps only if the steps help.
5. Show tools that implement the method.
6. End with what this changes in actual work.

The point is "now I can work differently", not "here is a complete methodology whitepaper".

### Trend Or Use-Case Observation

Use when discussing how people use AI or how a behavior is changing.

Natural movement:
1. Start with a concrete observation.
2. Move across several scenes or user groups.
3. Add light jokes or comparisons.
4. Extract one practical or social meaning.

Avoid becoming an industry report.

## Openings

Open from the concrete.

Good openings usually start with:
- a recent thing Max found
- a problem Max has
- a tool someone sent
- a chart or article that made a vague feeling clear
- a question Max wants to test

Avoid opening with the age, the industry, or the importance of AI.

## Headings And Lists

Max can use headings and lists. Do not ban them.

Use headings when they help the reader follow the test or product journey:
- RSS
- Codex 自动化
- Mac 应用
- 结尾

Use numbered lists when there are real problems, requirements, steps, or modes.

Do not add headings just to look structured. If a transition sentence is enough, use that.

## Images

Many source articles rely on screenshots. If images are absent:
- keep "图片" placeholders if the output is meant for公众号 draft
- otherwise briefly describe what the image would show
- do not over-explain every missing image

Screenshots usually serve as proof, rhythm breaks, or result display. Replace only the necessary context in words.

## Ending

Max endings are usually light.

Possible endings:
- a practical conclusion
- a limitation
- a next experiment
- a small hook
- a question for readers
- a simple "未完待续"

Avoid forced summary and forced elevation.

## Article Checklist

Before output, ask:
- Did the article start from a concrete problem or scene?
- Did it explain the tool/concept through use?
- Did it keep the shortest path to the point?
- Did it preserve a real reaction?
- Did it avoid turning into a tutorial, press release, or industry report?
- If it has a bigger thought, did that thought grow naturally from the material?
